Mathematics

Mathematics is the study of numbers, shapes and patterns. Mathematics is the science that deals with the logic of shape, quantity and arrangement. It's discovery has been at the forefront of every civilized society, and in use in even the most primitive of cultures. The needs of math arose based on the wants of society.The study of mathematics as a "demonstrative discipline" begins in the 6th century BC with the Pythagoreans, who coined the term "mathematics" from the ancient Greek μάθημα (mathema), meaning "subject of instruction".

Mathematics

This curriculum is designed to cover all the major topics like Algebra, Calculus , Probability & Statistics, Mensuration, Permutation and Combination, Co-ordinate geometry, Linear Programming, Relations and Functions etc.

  • Lecture 1: Relations and Functions

    An ordered pair is a set of inputs and outputs and represents a relationship between the two values. A relation is a set of inputs and outputs, and a function is a relation with one output for each input.

    • Lecture 1.1: Types of relations:

      Reflexive, symmetric, transitive and equivalence relations. One to one andonto functions, composite functions, inverse of a function.


      Language Translation
    • Lecture 1.2: Inverse Trigonometric Functions

      Definition, range, domain, principal value branch. Graphs of inverse trigonometric functions. Elementary properties of inverse trigonometric functions.


      Language Translation
  • Lecture 2:Calculus

    Calculus is a branch of mathematics which helps us understand changes between values that are related by a function. Calculus is used in many different areas such as physics, engineering, economics, medicine and sociology.

    • Lecture 2.1: Continuity and Differentiability

      Continuity and differentiability, derivative of composite functions, chain rule, derivative of inverse trigonometric functions derivative of implicit functions. Concept of exponential and logarithmic functions.Second order derivatives.


      Language Translation
    • Lecture 2.2: Integrals

      Periods Integration as inverse process of differentiation. Integration of a variety of functions by substitution, by partial fractions and by parts, Evaluation of simple integrals of the following types and problems based on them.


      Language Translation
  • Lecture 3:Algebra

    Mainly comprises of two major topics MAtries and Determinants where one will learn about different types matrices, Operations on them and about the properties of the Determinats.

  • Lecture 4: Linear Programming

    Periods Introduction, different types of (L.P.) problems, graphical method of solution for problems, feasible and infeasible solutions, optimal feasible solutions.

  • Lecture 5:Probability

    Conditional probability, multiplication theorem on probability, independent events, total probability, Bayes’ theorem, Random variable and its probability distribution, mean and variance of random variable.
